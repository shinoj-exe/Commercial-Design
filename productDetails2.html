Garlic

Garlic is an allium species that belongs to the lily family, and is closely related to onions, shallots, and chives. It grows underground in the form of a bulb, which is composed of individual sections called cloves that are used for both culinary and medicinal purposes. Garlic is a flavor powerhouse that brings the simplest foods to life—brushed with butter onto crusty slices of French bread, tossed into fresh pasta, and potatoes. Secondly, garlic can improve the health of your livestock, making pigs, chickens and cows as leading consumer, with many naturally-occurring properties, including antimicrobial, anti-parasitic, antiviral, and antioxidant-rich nutrients.
